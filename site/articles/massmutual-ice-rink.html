MassMutual Pittsburgh Ice Rink at PPG Place

Pittsburgh's extended winters offer a wide range of venues for public skating. However, one spectacular, one-of-a-kind destination stands out: the stunning ice rink constructed directly inside the gorgeous gothic glass spires of PPG Place.

The Experience

Officially the MassMutual Pittsburgh Ice Rink at PPG Place, this seasonal attraction usually opens on Light Up Night and operates through March 1st. During the summer, this identical space features a beautiful, synchronized water fountain.

The rink comfortably holds up to 350 people. It's an expansive 116′ x 116′ circular ice loop centered around a towering, beautifully decorated Christmas tree. Colored lights and holiday songs immediately turn the plaza into a cheerful winter wonderland.

Location & Surroundings

Located in the heart of Downtown between Third and Fourth Avenues, it sits directly in the shadow of One PPG Center. Just a minute away from Market Square, transitioning from ice skating to a warm dinner is incredibly easy. During the holiday season, you can also view the stunning local Gingerbread House displays inside the adjacent PPG Wintergarden.

A Note for Absolute Beginners

While taking a lap around PPG Place is incredibly picturesque, the ice conditions can get somewhat gouged due to heavy foot traffic entirely throughout the day. If you are lacing up skates for the absolute first time, prepare yourself for crowds and the occasional minor bump from a fellow skater. Despite these very minor inconveniences, the sheer ambiance of the glass castle surroundings makes it an unforgettable outing for the whole family.
