Pittsburgh Desi Guide Part 2

Building on our first guide, Part 2 explores the strategic advantages of living in Pittsburgh. For those eager to explore the East Coast, the city is perfectly positioned for rapid, inexpensive weekend trips to major historical destinations and cultural hubs.

Outbound Tourism: Exploring the East Coast

Pittsburgh serves as a phenomenal launching pad for short-term projects or groups looking to get the best "bang for their buck." Here are the easiest outbound excursions you can embark on over a weekend.

  • Washington D.C.: An incredibly convenient night trip. You can catch the MegaBus late on a Friday night (around 10:30 PM), arriving at D.C.'s Union Station just as the sun rises. We suggest utilizing the hop-on/hop-off tourist buses to take in the historical landmarks before catching an overnight bus back.
  • New York City: Similar to D.C., you can catch an overnight bus directly to Penn Station. While New York has a dizzying array of attractions, an aggressive day-trip is achievable and highly affordable.
  • Niagara Falls: A quintessential visit for the Indian community. Located just a 4-hour drive north of Pittsburgh, you can comfortably make this a single-day road trip. Plan ahead by purchasing tickets for the Maid of the Mist online to bypass the lines!

Spiritual Getaways: ISKCON New Vrindaban

Located in West Virginia, less than two hours south of Pittsburgh, New Vrindaban serves as a gorgeous sanctuary and major headquarters for the ISKCON movement. Packed with events year-round, you can witness beautiful temple architecture, ponds teeming with fish, and freely roaming peacocks displaying their plumage. A complimentary lunch is gracefully served at major events. Just be cautious driving the mountain roads during harsh winters.

Accommodation Hotspots for Indians

Consistently ranked as one of the most livable cities, Pittsburgh has specific enclaves where the Indian ecosystem thrives:

Green Tree Road: This corridor is historically the most popular starting point. You will find incredible proximity to Indian groceries (like Patel Brothers) and restaurants. Apartment complexes such as Carriage Park host one of the largest Indian communities in the region, ensuring you and your family are never short of friends. Easy public transit access to downtown makes this area incredibly convenient.

The Suburbs (Upper St. Clair, South Fayette, Wexford, Cranberry): As families grow, many transition to the northern or southern suburbs, seeking out top-rated school districts. Upper St. Clair is frequently cited as housing one of the best school systems in the state. While further from downtown and requiring a vehicle, the residential quality of life here is unmatched.
